About Me

Raised in Northern New Jersey, just outside of New York City, I started writing code in the 90’s. I worked as an avionics technician for 12 years before relocation to Washington. Officially, I switched careers to software a little over a decade ago where I spent several years working in startups where I was tasked with designing and building everything. Eventually I found my way into the corporate world, where I now spend most of my time somewhere between node.js express apps and front-end UI work. Typically ember.js or vue. Vue is really nice.

Tinkering

Currently, I’m working on building a self contained hydroponic home garden. Inside of a cabinet. It doesn’t end there. I’m developing a fully regulated environment, i.e., sensors will dictate when to heat or cool. Thanks to TensorFlow, it will use machine learning to send me a text if leaves start yellowing, or the plans somehow look unhealthy. Otherwise, the plan is to enjoy a nice time-lapse of basil growing. Well, that and making some fresh pesto.

Someday, I’ll turn it into a youtube video.
